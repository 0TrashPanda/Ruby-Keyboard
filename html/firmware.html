Firmware

We need firmware to run on our controller to be able to make it actually do something.
Depending on what firmware we choose, we will have to choose a matching microcontroller.
There already are different kinds of firmware that are specifically made for building custom keyboards.
We are not going to reinvent the wheel and thus will use them as our starting point.
There are 2 main options for what we want to do:

  • the QMK firmware witch is the oldest and the most popular.
  • and the ZMK firmware witch is like its little, unrelated brother.

QMK or ZMK

What is QMK

QMK (Quantum Mechanical Keyboard) is an open source firmware for computer input devices such as keyboards, mice, and MIDI devices.

Final decision

In the end, there was a clear winner: the ZMK firmware.
We choose ZMK because, even though there are a couple of features that we would like that are currently unsupported, they are already being worked on or planned. The wireless capability with the fastest implementation having less than 8ms of delay, battery efficiency and growing community together with the new and broadly available chips were also taken into account of course.
